My Adored Cat Breeds
Abyssinian Abyssinians are extremely clever and curious. They enjoy exploring and will not leave any nook or cranny unexplored. They are frequently referred to as "Aby-grabbys" because they steal items that catch their attention.
Siamese Siamese cats are gregarious, friendly, and intelligent animals that make wonderful companions. These kittens are described as "dog-like," and they fit in well with most households.
American Wirehair The American Wirehair cat makes an excellent companion. She is laid-back and calm. The Wirehair is a caring cat that will cling to every member of her household.
Patched Tabby Patched Tabbies feature areas of dark or grayish brown and patches of red or orange with the Tabby pattern running across both hues (thus the name). Tortoiseshell (or Tortie) Tabbies get its name from the brown and orange markings that resemble the shell of a tortoise.
Moggie Cat A moggie (also known as a domestic shorthair) is a non-pedigree cat of mixed or unknown heritage. They're the feline counterpart of a mongrel dog. They're pleasant, low-maintenance, and typically in good shape.
